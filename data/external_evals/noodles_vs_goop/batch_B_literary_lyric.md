# Noodles vs. Goop: Literary & Lyric Domains

## Analytical Frame

These three domains test the noodles/goop thesis against *aesthetic* rather than *normative* polysemy. In Domain 1 (Law, Halakha, Tafsīr), bounded multi-meaning produces doctrinal disagreement with institutional stakes — a wrong reading causes a wrong ruling. In the literary and lyric domains, bounded multi-meaning produces irreducible interpretive co-presence: the text means A and B simultaneously, not A or B depending on jurisdiction. The harm structure therefore shifts: there is no appeal body, no dissent that gets recorded, no identifiable "wrong ruling." The flattened output just *sounds like* a good reading of the text, and the failure is therefore even more invisible than in the legal or religious domains.

## Domain 1: Tang Regulated Verse (Lǜshī) — Du Fu, Li Bai

### 1. Polysemy Density: **Fractal**

The lǜshī (regulated verse) form, canonized in the Tang dynasty (618–907 CE), is arguably the densest polysemic engine in world literature per character. The form constrains every syllable by tonal pattern, every line by end-rhyme, and every couplet by semantic and grammatical parallelism — meaning that the formal substrate is not incidental but is itself the mechanism by which multiple meanings are held simultaneously. The polysemy operates fractally at every scale:

- **Character level**: Classical Chinese is uninflected and context-determines part of speech. A single character can simultaneously be noun, verb, and adjective depending on position. In Du Fu's *Chunwang* (春望, "Spring View"), the character 深 (*shēn*, "deep") in the line "grass and trees are deep" carries spatial depth, temporal depth (spring's advance), emotional depth (grief), and political depth (the Tang empire's wound) — all at once, and all load-bearing.
- **Line level**: The *duìzhàng* (对仗, antithetical parallelism) couplets of lǜshī require each line to mirror the other in grammatical structure, semantic category, and tonal pattern. This constraint means the *relationship* between the two parallel lines is itself a meaning-layer — a third reading generated by the tension between the paired images, which neither line produces alone.
- **Allusion level** (*diǎngù*, 典故): Tang poets encode literary, historical, and mythological references in standard phrases that activate entire prior texts when deployed. Stephen Owen's critical work on Du Fu demonstrates how single compound words in the lǜshī carry the weight of the specific *Book of Songs* or Han dynasty prose source they invoke — and that meaning is only available to readers who hold both the poem and its allusive substrate simultaneously.
- **Tonal pattern level**: The prescribed ping/zè (平/仄, level/oblique) tonal pattern governs not just sound but semantic register — the tonal texture of a couplet carries connotation about the emotional mode (elevation vs. heaviness) of the imagery. This is a phonological meaning-layer that simply does not exist in non-tonal translation environments.

Li Bai and Du Fu represent different poles of the same polysemic engineering: Li Bai's *piaoyi* style (飄逸, "ethereal") achieves simultaneous register through tonal lightness and mythological allusion-density; Du Fu's *chenyu duncuo* style (沈鬱頓挫, "profound-twisting") achieves it through syntactic compression and historical-political allusion deployed within tight formal constraints. The fractal structure is confirmed by the fact that every major classical Chinese commentary tradition — from the Song dynasty *shijing* notes to modern scholarly editions — produces irreconcilable readings of the same passages, and every irreconcilable reading is formally supportable.

### 2. Witness Tradition: **Institutionalized**

Tang poetry carries one of the most extensive witness traditions in world literature:

- The *Tangshi Sanbai Shou* (300 Tang Poems, compiled 1763) and its predecessors function as a canon with commentary infrastructure.
- Song dynasty annotators (such as the notes in the *Songben Du gongbu ji*) produced the first formal commentary chains on Du Fu, which subsequent commentators engaged, producing a chain of institutionalized disagreement traceable through the centuries.
- Stephen Owen's Harvard critical translations (particularly *The Great Age of Chinese Poetry: The High T'ang*, 1981, and the ongoing *The Poetry of Du Fu*, 6 vols., De Gruyter) represent the English-language witness apparatus, explicitly preserving interpretive alternatives and rejecting single-reading translation.
- The Chinese tradition maintains multiple competing schools of *shixue* (诗学, poetry-learning) that institutionalize their disagreements — some commentators read political allegory where others read natural imagery, and the tradition preserves both as simultaneously valid within the formal constraints of the text.

### 3. Current ML Treatment

- **ChatGPT, DeepL, Google Translate**: A 2024 *Humanities and Social Sciences Communications* study (Nature portfolio) compared these three on classical Chinese poetry translation, finding ChatGPT outperformed on "fidelity, fluency, language style" — but the evaluation criteria did not include allusion-layer fidelity or tonal-register preservation. ChatGPT was rated highly because the *surface* metrics are high, not because the polysemic depth is preserved.
- **WenyanGPT** (IJCAI 2025): A purpose-built LLM for classical Chinese, trained on 8B tokens of classical texts with fine-tuning for punctuation, translation, and "appreciation" tasks. Supports classical Chinese-to-modern Chinese and Chinese-to-English translation. Does not include allusion-database grounding or tonal-pattern preservation as evaluation criteria.
- **RAT (Retrieval-Augmented Translation)** (EMNLP 2025): Specifically addresses the *diǎngù* problem by augmenting LLM translation with retrieval from classical poetry knowledge bases. A benchmark study on LLM performance at translating classical Chinese poetry found that "existing LLMs fall short of this task" particularly on allusion handling, and proposed RAT as a mitigation. This is the most structurally aware approach in the current literature — but it treats allusion as a *fact* to be retrieved rather than a *multi-reading activation* to be preserved.
- **Qwen, ERNIE Bot, Ding Wangdao** (Chinese commercial LLMs): Compared on a classical literature translation task in 2024. Qwen translated "peach forest" (*táolín*) as "grove of trees" — erasing the specific literary-cultural resonance of the peach forest motif (Tao Yuanming's *Peach Blossom Spring* utopia) that the original image activates.
- **Sentiment analysis on Tang-Song poetry corpus** (arXiv 2025): Applied sentiment analysis and visual generation to Tang-Song poetry corpora, producing emotional classification tags. The paper treats the poems as sentiment carriers without acknowledging that the same poem regularly receives irreconcilable emotional classifications from classical commentators.
- **City University of Hong Kong corpus-based study** (2025): Corpus-based computational comparison of Li Bai and Du Fu across sound, sentiment, and style — using sound symbolism analysis, GloVe embeddings, and sentiment scoring. The study produces quantifiable stylistic differentiation but explicitly operates at a level where the multi-reading structure of individual poems is invisible.

### 4. Cooking Test Verdict: **Yes**

Dense embedding loses the allusion layer completely: a *diǎngù* compound encodes a specific prior text as part of its meaning, and this inter-textual activation is not recoverable from co-occurrence statistics because the referenced texts are too distant from the embedding training distribution of any model trained primarily on modern text. A model that scores "peach forest" as "nature imagery" has read the character without reading the poem.

### 5. Concrete Harm Vector

A researcher or student uses WenyanGPT or RAT to translate Du Fu's *Qiuxi* (秋興, "Autumn Meditations," 8-poem sequence), considered the peak of lǜshī complexity. The AI translation is technically well-formed — the tones approximate stress patterns in English, the parallelism is approximately preserved, the surface imagery is correct. But the allusive sub-structure is gone: the recurring *diǎngù* from the *Shijing* (Book of Songs), from Qu Yuan's *Lisao*, and from Han dynasty memorials that Du Fu activates in specific couplets to comment on the An Lushan Rebellion as a recurrence of dynastic collapse — this historical-political argument, carried entirely by allusion rather than by direct statement, becomes invisible. The translation reads as beautiful nature poetry. Scholars trained on the translation enter the *Qiuxi* critical literature without knowing that the debate about Du Fu's political allegory in this sequence exists, and AI-assisted literary study of Tang poetry in English-language institutions produces a generation of scholarship on the wrong text.

### 6. Falsifiable Prediction

By 2028, a preregistered benchmark of LLM translation quality on 20 Tang lǜshī known to contain high *diǎngù* density — evaluated by a panel of Sinologists scoring specifically for allusion-layer activation, tonal register preservation, and parallel couplet relational meaning — will find that top-performing models (including RAT-augmented systems) score below 30% on allusion-layer criteria even when scoring above 70% on fluency and surface fidelity. The 30% threshold is calibrated against the known failure rate of non-specialist human translators on the same texts. Falsified if RAT-augmented models consistently score above 50% on allusion-layer criteria across multiple evaluator panels.

### 7. Substrate-Fit Score: **8/10**

The witness tradition is institutionalized and partially digitized (Chinese classical text databases, Owen's critical editions). The polysemy layers are formally enumerable: tonal pattern, parallelism structure, *diǎngù* source, commentarial school. A polysemy-preserving substrate could tag each character with its tonal value and allusive references, link to the source texts activated by each *diǎngù*, and present alternative readings from the major commentarial schools as parallel tracks. Two points deducted: (1) the tonal-register meaning layer cannot be rendered in non-tonal target languages — the substrate can preserve it but the rendering problem has no solution for English audiences; (2) the parallelism relational-meaning layer (the third meaning produced by the *tension* between the two parallel lines) is an emergent property that cannot be stored as a node — it requires a reader who holds both lines simultaneously. The substrate can annotate, but the cooking cannot be replicated from the recipe.

## Domain 2: Anglophone Song Lyric — Leonard Cohen, Bob Dylan, Joni Mitchell

### 1. Polysemy Density: **High** (approaching fractal at the song level; fractal across the oeuvre)

The crafted Anglophone lyric of the Cohen/Dylan/Mitchell tradition operates differently from the domains analyzed in Prompt 1: the polysemy is not primarily doctrine-bounded or formally-rule-bounded but *craft-bounded* — held in tension by the interaction of lyric text with melody, harmonic movement, vocal delivery, and the specific cultural-biographical-intertextual context the listener brings. This makes the polysemy less formally enumerable but no less real or intentional.

Exemplary cases:

- **Leonard Cohen's "Hallelujah"** (1984, with revisions through 2004): Cohen famously wrote 80+ draft verses and maintained multiple competing published versions of the song, explicitly stating he "rewrote this song; this is the 'secular Hallelujah'". The word "Hallelujah" itself operates simultaneously as Hebrew liturgical praise, as ironic self-excoriation (the "broken hallelujah" of the final verse), as a statement about the relationship between the sacred and the erotic, and as a formal-musical term (the long melismatic hold on the final syllable). The biblical references (David/Bathsheba, Samson/Delilah) are not decorative — they are part of the argument, and the argument is simultaneously a love song, a theological meditation on human failure, and a craft statement about composition itself ("a baffled king composing"). The song has been recorded 300+ times by different artists, each of whom resolve the ambiguity differently — a measurable witness tradition of interpretive divergence.
- **Bob Dylan's layered irony**: Paul Simon's observation that "everything [Dylan] sings has two meanings — he's telling you the truth and making fun of you at the same time" is not a stylistic quirk but a structural design principle. Dylan's Nobel Prize was awarded "for having created new poetic expressions within the great American song tradition" — recognition that the lyric is a designed literary object. Dylan explicitly states in his Nobel lecture that *Moby-Dick*, *The Odyssey*, and *All Quiet on the Western Front* were foundational to his lyric composition practice — meaning the allusive depth is not accidental.
- **Joni Mitchell's layered autobiography**: Mitchell's "A Case of You" (1971), "The Last Time I Saw Richard" (1971), and "Coyote" (1976) operate through a structured ambiguity between the autobiographical-literal and the universalised-archetypal that she has repeatedly described as deliberate — the personal detail *is* the universal claim, not an illustration of it.

At the oeuvre level, the polysemy becomes fractal: individual songs allude to and modify each other (Cohen's use of biblical narrative across his career constitutes an ongoing argument with the texts he is citing), and the versions of a single song over time (Cohen's revisions of "Hallelujah," Dylan's live reinterpretations of "Tangled Up in Blue") constitute an ongoing commentary tradition generated by the author.

### 2. Witness Tradition: **Curated**

The witness tradition for this domain is real but structurally different from the institutionalized traditions of law and religion:

- **Genius.com**: 5+ million annotated songs, with crowdsourced line-by-line annotations that constitute a lay witness tradition — preserving interpretive disagreement at the line level, but with no adjudication mechanism and no school-affiliation structure.
- **Academic literary criticism**: The Dylan/Cohen/Mitchell critical literature is substantial — biographies (Sounes on Dylan, Simmons on Cohen), specialized journals (*Dylan Review*, *A Journal of Bob Dylan Studies*), and the scholarly reception of Dylan's Nobel has generated a peer-reviewed literature on the literary status of song lyric.
- **The song's own revision history as witness**: Cohen's multiple published versions of "Hallelujah" constitute a formal witness tradition in which the author adjudicates his own disagreements across time — uniquely, the primary text is itself its own commentary layer.

The tradition is "curated" rather than "institutionalized" because: there is no formal adjudication apparatus; the interpretive disagreements are not jurisdiction-specific; and the tradition is geographically distributed rather than institutionally anchored.

### 3. Current ML Treatment

- **Musixmatch AI / LyricIQ**: Musixmatch's AI platform (Lyrics2Vec, Artist2Vec) extracts "entities, explicitness, sentiment, emotions and topics" from lyrics, building personalized playlist recommendation systems. LyricIQ applies IBM Watson's Ekman Atlas of Emotions framework to lyrics for sentiment scoring and content categorization across 31 categories (religion, sex, violence, etc.). These systems operate on the lyric as a sentiment-carrier, not as a polysemic object.
- **Musixmatch Lyric Lens API**: Generates a "simple, short explanation of the lyrics' content" — explicitly a *flattening* operation, as the documentation illustrates with a one-sentence summary of Miley Cyrus's "Flowers". Applied to "Hallelujah," this produces a one-sentence summary that picks one reading of the song's irresolvable theology/erotic/craft triple-bind.
- **LyricLens (Music Smatch)**: Production AI system processing millions of songs for "semantic meaning, themes, entities, cultural references" — described as a "multi-level semantic engine" operating a "knowledge graph structure" of concept relationships. Designed to enable brand targeting, recommendation, and demographic analysis. Treats cultural references as facts to be extracted, not as polysemic activations.
- **SongMeaning AI**: Lyric interpretation tool offering "line-by-line breakdown" with "sentiment and mood detection" — generates "clear annotations that explain references, metaphors, and shifts in tone". The word "clear" in the product description is the tell: clarity is achieved by resolving the ambiguity.
- **Computational songwriting analysis** (Digital Studies, 2026): A computational framework for "analyzing how popular songwriting encodes and reflects cultural memory" — applies embedding models to song corpora to extract cultural memory patterns across time. The method is inherently averaging: cultural memory is the output of a similarity-cluster, not a multi-reading activation.
- **AI style imitation**: Multiple products (and the American Songwriter demonstration) generate lyrics "in the style of Bob Dylan and Leonard Cohen". These outputs are fluent, approximate the surface markers of each style (Dylan: elliptical imagery + harmonica metaphors; Cohen: biblical reference + romantic candlelight), and miss the structural polysemy entirely — as demonstrated by the sample output in the American Songwriter piece, which produces images without irony and biblical reference without the erotic/sacred tension that makes Cohen's use of those references operative.

### 4. Cooking Test Verdict: **Yes**

Dense embedding loses the irony layer, which is structurally irreducible: irony requires simultaneously holding "what is said" and "what is meant" in tension, and the averaging operation resolves this to the semantic centroid of what the text says. Research confirms that "irony and sarcasm detection often remains unreliable and that performance degrades sharply when conversations involve cultural subtleties" — and LLM irony detection is weaker in poetry and lyric than in direct speech because the disambiguating cues (facial expression, tone of voice) are absent.

### 5. Concrete Harm Vector

A music discovery platform using Musixmatch Lyric Lens or a similar API tags "Hallelujah" with sentiment = "melancholic/spiritual," theme = "faith and loss," content categories = "religion" and "romance." These tags are then used to power recommendation and content placement algorithms, to train new AI generation models, and — most concretely — to adjudicate licensing disputes and content moderation decisions (e.g., whether the song is "religious content" for platform categorization purposes that affect monetization). The specific harm: the song's *constitutive ambiguity* between sacred and secular — which is Cohen's explicit artistic achievement, documented in his own statements about writing a "secular hallelujah" — is resolved to a single tag, and every downstream system trained on or using that tag treats the resolution as factual. Cohen's theological argument (that the holy and the broken are the same word) is silently erased from the system's understanding of the song and from the generation models trained on that understanding.

### 6. Falsifiable Prediction

By 2028, an empirical study comparing AI lyric analysis tools (Musixmatch, Genius AI, SongMeaning AI) against expert literary critics on 50 songs identified in the musicological literature as structurally ironic, multi-register, or ambiguous in their primary affective frame will find that AI tools fail to identify the *operative* reading (the one supported by the artist's documented compositional intent and critical consensus) in >60% of cases, instead producing a "majority semantic" reading that collapses the structural ambiguity. Measurable against: artist interviews, Nobel Prize citations, published critical monographs, and the documented variation in covers/interpretations as a proxy for the text's live multi-reading architecture. Falsified if AI tools score above 60% on irony/ambiguity identification *without* being specifically fine-tuned on irony-labeled literary data.

### 7. Substrate-Fit Score: **6/10**

The substrate-fit is real but lower than the legal and religious domains because: (1) the witness tradition, while genuine, is not institutionalized — there is no formal per-school rendering apparatus to map onto; (2) the polysemy is partly constituted by the *interaction* of lyric with music, and a text-only substrate cannot preserve the meaning-layer produced by Cohen's harmonic choices or Dylan's vocal delivery; (3) the revision history as self-commentary is a unique asset (Cohen's multiple "Hallelujah" versions could be preserved as a per-version rendering layer), and this would add genuine value. Four points deducted for the music-text interaction problem (which is structural, not solvable by annotation) and the non-institutional witness tradition (which limits the per-school rendering option).

## Domain 3: Modernist Literary Layers — Moby-Dick, Beloved, Hamlet, Ulysses

### 1. Polysemy Density: **Fractal**

These texts share a single structural property: they were *engineered* to sustain irreconcilable interpretive readings across centuries. The fractal character is demonstrable at every scale:

- **Hamlet**: At the word level, "To be or not to be" has generated 400 years of irreconcilable critical debate about whether it is a question about suicide, about action, about ontology, or about theatrical self-reference. At the play level, the question of whether Hamlet's delay is psychological, strategic, or metaphysical has produced mutually exclusive critical schools (Romantic Hamlet, Oedipal Hamlet, political Hamlet, deconstructive Hamlet) that each have full textual support. James Joyce quoted approvingly: "I've included so many enigmas and puzzles that it will keep the professors occupied for centuries debating my intentions, and that's the only method of securing one's immortality" — a structural design principle, not an accident.
- **Moby-Dick**: Melville's novel simultaneously operates as a naturalist's account of whaling, an allegory of American exceptionalism and its self-destruction, a Gnostic cosmological argument, a Shakespearean tragedy, a meditation on race and labor, and a text about the impossibility of reading (the Spouter-Inn painting chapter is a meditation on the act of interpretation itself). The Shakespearean resonance is embedded at the syntactic level (the formal language of Ahab's speeches), the structural level (the chapter organization mirrors Lear and Macbeth), and the allusive level. Bob Dylan's Nobel lecture on Moby-Dick demonstrates how a reader trained in one tradition (American folk song) reads it as an entirely different text from a reader trained in academic literary criticism — both readings fully supportable.
- **Beloved**: Toni Morrison's novel operates simultaneously as historical realism, ghost story, psychological trauma narrative, and mythological invocation (the "rememory" concept as ontological category, not psychological metaphor). Morrison's own stated principle — that she wanted to "kidnap" the audience and disorient them as slavery disoriented the enslaved — is a design commitment to preventing stable resolution. The poststructuralist literature on *Beloved* demonstrates how the novel systematically refuses its own terms: the mother/murderer identity, the alive/dead status of Beloved, the past/present temporal structure. An AI that resolves these into a "Gothic trauma novel" has flattened the ontological argument of the text.
- **Ulysses**: Joyce's structural principle — encoding an entire field of interpretation (Homeric, psychological, theological, linguistic) into a single day's narrative — produces a text that the Stanford Literary Lab's own network analysis demonstrates is resistant to computational reduction: "A lot goes by the wayside in this transformation, including the content of those exchanges and all of Hamlet's soliloquies (i.e., all interior experience)". A Reddit reader's account of using ChatGPT to interpret *Ulysses* is telling: "I discovered that AI was not much better at it than I am. Whenever I posited a particular theory, I found that it invariably (and enthusiastically) agreed with me and began expanding on my own train of thought. So, no — it's not going to tell you what the book actually MEANS!!".

### 2. Witness Tradition: **Institutionalized**

The witness tradition for the Western literary canon is the most extensive in existence and the most institutionalized outside law:

- **Hamlet**: 400 years of theatrical production, critical commentary (Coleridge, Goethe, Bradley, Freud, Derrida, Cavell), and academic scholarship constitute an institutionalized disagreement structure. The Arden, Folger, and Oxford critical editions preserve textual variants (the First Quarto / Second Quarto / First Folio problem, where three versions of the play exist with materially different texts) as primary evidence that the text's multi-reading character goes to the source.
- **Moby-Dick**: The *Norton Critical Edition* tradition, JSTOR database of scholarship, and specialized organizations (Melville Society, its journal *Leviathan*) maintain and curate the disagreement structure.
- **Beloved**: The Morrison scholarship is dense and explicitly preserves its irreconcilabilities — the psychoanalytic, poststructuralist, Black feminist, and historical-materialist schools each produce incompatible readings that the academic apparatus preserves as co-valid.
- **Ulysses**: The witness tradition is itself a cultural institution — the James Joyce Foundation, the *James Joyce Quarterly*, the Joyce Studies Annual, and the scholarly apparatus of annotation (Don Gifford's *Ulysses Annotated*) constitute a formalized multi-reading preservation system that is among the most developed for any single text.

### 3. Current ML Treatment

- **Stanford Literary Lab (Franco Moretti / Mark Algee-Hewitt)**: The Lab's foundational "distant reading" methodology operates on large corpora, treating individual texts as instances of patterns. The Lab's network analysis of *Hamlet* (Moretti, Literary Lab Pamphlet 2) explicitly discards "all interior experience" and "all of Hamlet's soliloquies" in order to produce a character-network graph. Moretti's own work acknowledges this sacrifice — but the Lab's methodology has been broadly adopted by digital humanities researchers who do not always acknowledge what is lost.
- **ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini applied to literary analysis**: The *JALT Journal* essay on *Ulysses* and AI found that when a reader uses ChatGPT to interpret the novel, "it invariably agrees with whatever the user proposes" — the system's averaging operation means it has no stable interpretive position and simply reinforces whatever reading is presented to it. This is a particular failure mode for Ulysses because the text is *designed* to simultaneously support multiple contradictory readings: the AI's agreement is not evidence of understanding but of having no structural commitment to any reading.
- **AI vs. Close Reading (comparative study on Virginia Woolf)**: A published comparative study found that AI "lacks: figurative sensitivity (unable to fully decode metaphor or irony) [and] context awareness (misses historical, gendered, and existential subtext)" in close reading of literary texts.
- **AI applied to Beloved scholarship**: Research at the intersection of AI and Black feminist epistemology (2024, IJRPR) explicitly argues that algorithmic systems reproduce "the myth of neutrality" when applied to texts like Morrison's, treating digitized archives as representative data rather than "constructed political artifacts". The paper proposes "Corrective AI" as a framework — acknowledging that the current ML treatment of *Beloved* produces readings that reinforce rather than challenge the archival violence embedded in the primary sources.
- **Public Domain Review + GPT-3 experiment on Moby-Dick**: A documented experiment (Eigil zu Tage-Ravn) applied GPT-3 to the Spouter-Inn painting chapter of *Moby-Dick* — one of the novel's most self-consciously polysemic passages (it is about the act of interpretation itself). The AI produced a competent paraphrase that missed the self-referential argument entirely, treating the chapter as setting description rather than meta-commentary.
- **LLM-generated literary analysis in education**: The SCIRP paper (2025) documents that AI tools are "fraught with ethical and methodological challenges" in literary studies, specifically citing misinterpretation of irony, symbolism, and cultural context in "postcolonial texts" — but the finding applies equally to canonical texts where the polysemy is structural rather than cultural.

### 4. Cooking Test Verdict: **Yes (most severely for Ulysses and Beloved)**

Dense embedding loses the constitutive irresolution of these texts: an AI summary of *Beloved* that tells you it is "a ghost story about the trauma of slavery" has replaced the novel's central argument (that the categories of ghost, person, memory, and history are not separable) with a genre label. For *Ulysses*, the failure is definitional — Joyce's stated design principle was to produce a text that cannot be definitively resolved, and an AI that produces a coherent summary has, by that fact, failed to read the text.

### 5. Concrete Harm Vector

An LLM-powered literary analysis tool deployed in secondary and university education (multiple platforms now offer AI-assisted essay preparation — Chegg's Chegg-Write, Course Hero's AI, Turnitin's AI feedback tools) generates an analysis of *Hamlet*'s Act III Scene 1 soliloquy for a student essay. The AI produces a sophisticated-sounding close reading that identifies the soliloquy as "a meditation on the fear of death as the source of Hamlet's inaction." This reading is textually supportable — but it is one reading among five or six irreconcilable critical positions (Bradley's psychological reading, Cavell's skeptical reading, Derrida's spectrality reading, the psychoanalytic reading, the political reading of the King's surveillance of Hamlet). The student's essay cites the AI-generated reading as if it were critical consensus. The essay is evaluated by a professor (or an AI rubric system), receives a good grade for being "well-supported," and the student exits the course having learned that *Hamlet* has a correct interpretation — having been taught the opposite of what the play is designed to teach.

### 6. Falsifiable Prediction

By 2027, a study of AI-assisted literary analysis in undergraduate education will find that students who use AI tools for essay preparation on *Hamlet*, *Beloved*, and *Ulysses* produce essays that cite single interpretive positions as critical consensus (rather than acknowledging irreconcilable critical schools) at a significantly higher rate than students who use traditional scholarship methods, as measured against a rubric coding for "acknowledgment of interpretive plurality." The effect will be measurable and statistically significant (p < 0.05) with a sample of 200+ essays per text per condition. Falsified if no significant difference is found, or if AI-assisted students produce *more* acknowledgment of interpretive plurality — which would require AI tools to have been redesigned to present multiple critical readings rather than resolving to a synthesis.

### 7. Substrate-Fit Score: **9/10**

The canonical literary texts are the second-best use case for a polysemy-preserving substrate, after Halakha. The witness tradition is institutionalized (critical editions, specialized journals, annotated bibliographies). The interpretive schools are identifiable and named (Romantic Hamlet, Oedipal Hamlet, Deconstructive Hamlet). The textual variants are formally documented (the three-text problem of *Hamlet*). A substrate could render per-school interpretations in parallel, link each interpretive claim to the specific critical tradition that supports it, and present irreconcilable readings as co-valid rather than resolving them to a synthesis. One point deducted for the Ulysses problem: Joyce's deliberate design of the text to resist any stable interpretation means that a substrate which claims to preserve *all* interpretations simultaneously is itself making an interpretive claim (that the text is "about" plurality) that some critical schools would dispute. The meta-level polysemy of *Ulysses* is not a solvable annotation problem.

## Cross-Domain Synthesis (Engine 2): Literary & Lyric vs. Normative Domains

| Dimension | Tang Lǜshī | Anglophone Lyric | Modernist Fiction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Polysemy density | Fractal | High → fractal at oeuvre level | Fractal (designed) |
| Witness tradition | Institutionalized | Curated | Institutionalized |
| Primary ML actors | WenyanGPT, RAT, ChatGPT, Qwen | Musixmatch, LyricIQ, SongMeaning AI | Stanford Lit Lab, ChatGPT, edu-AI tools |
| Cooking test | Yes (allusion layer) | Yes (irony layer) | Yes (constitutive irresolution) |
| Harm onset | Active (Tang studies) | Active (platform tagging) | Active (education) |
| Substrate-fit score | 8/10 | 6/10 | 9/10 |

The key structural difference from the Prompt 1 domains: there is no institutional adjudication mechanism. A wrong Fourth Amendment analysis can be corrected by a court; a wrong halakhic ruling can be appealed to a beit din. A wrong reading of "Hallelujah" or *Hamlet* has no institutional correction mechanism — the wrong reading simply propagates through educational systems, recommendation algorithms, and generation-model training pipelines, becoming the ground truth by repetition.

The irony layer is the sharpest failure point across all three literary domains. LLM systems are stochastic pattern-completion systems — they generate the most statistically probable continuation of the text, which is to say the most common reading. Irony, by definition, requires holding the common reading and its negation simultaneously. Texts that are long-lived *because* they sustain irony (Hamlet's "I loved Ophelia" speech, Dylan's "everything I say is true and a joke simultaneously", Cohen's "broken hallelujah as sacred as the holy hallelujah") are texts that ML systems will most reliably flatten. The long-lived texts are disproportionately ironic texts. The ML failure is therefore not random — it is *systematically aimed at the most load-bearing noodles*.

## ENGINE 1 RESPONSE — Batch B

### Domain 1: Tang regulated verse (lǜshī) — Du Fu, Li Bai, tonal-pattern + parallelism + allusion-density tradition
